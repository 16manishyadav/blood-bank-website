About Us

  • There are 4 main blood groups: A, B, AB and O.
  • Each blood group can be either Rh positive or Rh negative.
  • There are 8 different blood types: A+, A-, B+, B-, AB+, AB-, O+ and O-.
  • The universal donor is

    O-

    who can give blood to anyone, but can only receive blood from another universal donor.
  • The universal recipient is

    AB+

    who can receive blood from anyone, but can only give blood to another universal recipient.
  • Age: The minimum age for blood donation is usually 17 or 18 years, depending on the country and blood bank. Some blood banks may require donors to be older than 18 years.
  • Weight: Donors must weigh at least 110 pounds or 50 kilograms.
  • Health: Donors must be in good health, without any major health conditions that could affect the safety of the blood supply or the donor's health.
  • Medications: Donors must not be taking certain medications, such as blood thinners, that could affect the safety of the blood supply.
  • Lifestyle: Donors must not engage in high-risk behaviors that could increase their risk of transmitting infectious diseases, such as having unprotected sex, using illegal drugs, or traveling to certain high-risk areas.
  • Travel: Donors must not have traveled to certain high-risk areas or countries within a specific period of time, typically ranging from 3 to 12 months, depending on the country and blood bank.
