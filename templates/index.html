Some Cool Facts About Blood Donation

  • Platelets, a component of blood, are crucial for cancer patients undergoing
  • Blood donation not only helps the recipients, but also has health benefits for the donors. It can reduce the risk of heart diseases, stroke, and cancer.
  • The human body contains about 5.5 liters of blood, but during a donation, only about 470ml is collected, which is less than 10% of the total volume.
  • The average adult has about 10 pints of blood in their body, and a single pint of blood can help save up to three lives.

Why Blood Donation Is Important?

  • Saves lives: Blood donation is critical in providing life-saving treatments to those in need. It is used to support surgeries, medical procedures, and treatments for patients with serious illnesses and injuries.
  • No substitute: Blood cannot be manufactured, and there is no substitute for it. It can only come from donors, making the need for regular blood donation crucial.
  • National Blood Supply: Blood donations are essential in maintaining the National Blood Supply, which is a critical resource for hospitals and medical facilities.
  • Natural disasters and emergencies: Blood donation is especially important during natural disasters and emergencies when the demand for blood can increase rapidly.
